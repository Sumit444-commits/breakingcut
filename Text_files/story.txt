Rain lashed against the bakery window, blurring the warm glow inside. Old Man Fitzwilliam, hunched over his counter, watched a small girl with a patched coat shiver outside. He recognized her; she often peered in, her nose pressed to the glass, eyes wide with longing at the sugared buns. He sighed. Business had been bad lately. Still, he couldn't leave her out there. He opened the door, the aroma of yeast and cinnamon swirling into the stormy air. "Come in, child," he said, his voice gruff but kind. "Come in and have a bun. On the house." The girl's eyes widened, and a hesitant smile bloomed on her face as she stepped inside, the warmth chasing away the cold.
