The old lighthouse keeper, Silas, whose beard resembled tangled seaweed, squinted at the approaching storm, a malevolent canvas of bruised purple and angry grey, and knew, with a sinking heart, that the legend of the siren's song, a melody said to lure sailors to their doom on the jagged rocks below, was about to be tested once more, not just on passing ships, but on his very sanity, as the wind began to whisper a haunting tune that seemed to pluck at the deepest, loneliest chords of his soul.
